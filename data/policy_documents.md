## Policy P-12345: Standard Auto Coverage

**Policy Number:** P-12345  
**Effective Date:** 2024-01-01 to 2025-01-01  
**Policyholder:** Jane Doe  
**Vehicle:** 2020 Toyota Camry, VIN: 1234567890ABCDEFG  

### Coverage Summary

**Bodily Injury Liability:** $100,000 per person / $300,000 per accident  
**Property Damage Liability:** $50,000 per accident  
**Collision Coverage:** $500 deductible  
**Comprehensive Coverage:** $250 deductible  
**Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist:** $100,000 per person / $300,000 per accident  
**Medical Payments:** $5,000 per person  

### Personal Vehicle Use Only
This policy covers personal use of the insured vehicle. Commercial use, including but not limited to:
- Food delivery (pizza, groceries, restaurant meals)
- Ridesharing services (Uber, Lyft, etc.)
- Package/courier delivery
- For-hire transportation
is **NOT COVERED** under this policy.

Any accident occurring while the vehicle is being used for commercial purposes will result in **claim denial**.

### Total Loss Threshold
If repair costs exceed 75% of the vehicle's actual cash value (ACV), the claim will be processed as a total loss. In such cases:
- The insurer will pay the vehicle's ACV minus the applicable deductible
- The insured must surrender the vehicle title
- No repair will be authorized

### Pre-Existing Damage
Any damage that existed prior to the policy effective date or prior to the reported loss is excluded from coverage. The appraiser will assess whether damage is consistent with the reported incident.

## Fraud Detection Guidelines (Internal Use)

### Red Flags Requiring SIU Referral

**Accident Circumstances:**
- Late reporting (more than 72 hours after incident)
- Inconsistent statements between police report and FNOL
- No independent witnesses in parking lot or low-traffic areas
- Claimant or passengers have history of multiple claims
- Soft tissue injuries with no visible vehicle damage

**Vehicle Damage Patterns:**
- Pre-existing damage inconsistent with reported incident
- Damage location doesn't match impact description
- Vehicle value significantly less than repair estimate (potential total loss inflation)
- VIN tampering or salvage title not disclosed

**Financial Indicators:**
- Claimant recently increased coverage limits (within 30 days of loss)
- Claimant has outstanding loan balance far exceeding vehicle value
- Claimant requests direct payment instead of repair shop assignment
- Multiple claims across different insurers in short timeframe

### Bodily Injury Red Flags

**Medical Treatment Patterns:**
- Treatment begins weeks after accident
- Visits only to chiropractors/physical therapy (no ER or primary care)
- Treatment continues beyond typical recovery period
- Provider is known for high claim volumes
- Claimant has attorney involvement before medical diagnosis

**Litigation Risk Factors:**
- Attorney representation within 48 hours of loss
- Demand letters sent before medical treatment concludes
- Pre-litigation demands exceed policy limits
- Claimant has history of personal injury lawsuits

## Subrogation Policy

### When to Pursue Subrogation

**We will pursue subrogation recovery when:**
- Policyholder is 0% at fault (not-at-fault collision)
- Other party is identified with valid insurance
- Claim payout exceeds $2,500
- Police report assigns fault to other party
- Potential recovery exceeds cost of pursuit (minimum $5,000)

**Subrogation Process:**
1. Pay policyholder's collision claim (waive deductible if other party at fault)
2. Send demand letter to at-fault party's insurer
3. Negotiate settlement or file arbitration
4. Reimburse policyholder's deductible if full recovery obtained
5. Recover claim payout amount

**We will NOT pursue subrogation if:**
- Comparative negligence exceeds 25%
- Other party is uninsured with no assets
- Claim amount below $2,500
- Statute of limitations has expired
- Other party is household member or family

## Total Loss Valuation Guidelines

### Determining Actual Cash Value (ACV)

**ACV Calculation Formula:**
ACV = Comparable Vehicle Market Value - Pre-Existing Damage - Excessive Wear/Tear

**Comparable Vehicle Criteria:**
- Same year, make, model, trim level
- Within 10,000 miles of subject vehicle mileage
- Similar condition (clean title, no major repairs)
- Within 100-mile radius of loss location
- Sold within 45 days of loss date

**Pre-Existing Damage Deductions:**
- Prior accident damage not repaired: Deduct estimated repair cost
- Rust, body damage, mechanical issues: Deduct depreciation
- Missing or non-functional features: Deduct replacement cost

**Total Loss Settlement:**
1. Determine ACV using 3-5 comparable vehicles
2. Deduct applicable deductible
3. Deduct salvage value (if insured retains vehicle)
4. Pay net settlement amount
5. Require title transfer (if salvage not retained)
